Rubik's Cube and Subgroups

Cube Notation

Rubik's Cube: composed of 27 small cubies.

Cubie: 1×1×11 \times 1 \times 11×1×1﻿ small cube.

Visible —> 26 cubies

Referring to cubies:

Corner cubies —> 3 visible faces, 8 in number

Edge cubies —> 2 visible faces, 12 in number

Center cubies —> 1 visible face, 6 in number

Referring to faces of cube:

  1. Right (r)
  1. Left (l)
  1. Up (u)
  1. Down (d)
  1. Front (f)
  1. Back (b)

Naming a corner cubie:

List its visible faces in clockwise order.

For example, cubie in the upper, right, front corner is written urf.

Naming edge and center cubies:

List the visible faces of the cubies in clockwise order.

Cubicles: Labeled same way as cubies but they describe the space where a cubie lives in. This, if rubick's cube is in the start configuration (solved), then each cubie lives in the cubicle of the same name.

For example: urf cubie lives in the urf cubicle at start configuration.

Moves of a rubik's cube:

Basic move —> Rotate a single face

RRR﻿ : clockwise rotation of the right face (looking at right face, turn it clockwise)

L,U,D,F,BL, U, D, F, BL,U,D,F,B﻿ denote clockwise twists corresponding to their faces.

Move —> Sequence of these 6 face twists

Observations:

These six basic moves determine every possible move in a rubik's cube.

A cubie cannot change its type of cubicle. That is, an edge cubie always remains in an edge cubicle, center cubie in a center cubicle and corner cubie in a corner cubicle.
