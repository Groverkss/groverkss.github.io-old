Multiple Random Variables

Concepts done in Joint Random Variables can be easily extended to more than two variables. But as the number of variables grows, it quickly becomes computationally intractable to work with these functions. Dealing with multiple random variables, we resort to alternate techniques such as moment generating functions and probabilistic bounds.
